Exterior Products

In our quest to generalise calculus, we must figure out the most general objects to apply it to - which mathematical entities satisfy just enough conditions so that we can define useful derivatives and integrals?

The motivation here is absolutely paramount. We understand that $1$-forms can help generalise the derivative and integral, but to go the distance we need some way of combining forms: \[\int \int xy dx dy,\] for example, hints that we need to have some "product" of forms which is still integrable.
